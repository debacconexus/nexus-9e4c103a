📋 Original Problem Statement
Professional Learning and Development System for Special Education in Inclusive Classrooms across Belizean schools and California inner-city and charter schools. Each educator record tracks 12 fields: Module Completion Status tracking which of 6 PLD modules completed plus engagement level, Post-Training Confidence Rating on a 1-10 scale, Classroom Observation Score from external observer rubric rating, Student IEP Progress Measure tracking whether students with IEPs are progressing, Implementation Fidelity Level measuring how consistently educators use PLD strategies to separate confidence from actual behavior change, Regional Compliance Framework documenting IDEA for US or Belize-specific protocols, Educator Experience Level capturing years teaching plus special ed certification plus prior PD, Baseline Confidence Score Pre-Intervention for measuring change, Specific Practices Changed tracking which strategies stuck including differentiation and peer support and assistive tech and behavior management, PD Module Attendance tracking completion rate and engagement signals, Student Population Served documenting disability types and severity levels and baseline achievement, and Support Structure Quality measuring coaching availability and peer collaboration and resource access and school leadership buy-in. Stakeholders include teachers themselves, school administrators, the research team, special education directors in both regions, UCLA Digital Technology and Solutions, Belize National Teachers Union, and the US Embassy in Belize for funding accountability. Compliance frameworks include IDEA, research ethics protocols for US versus Belize contexts, BNTU professional learning standards, and school accountability measures. The 6 PLD modules are Special Education Coping Mechanisms for Educators, Classroom Management in Inclusive Settings, AI and Gamification in Special Education, Instructional Technology and Integration, Computer Studies for Accessibility, and Financial Literacy for Educators. Governed notes must capture classroom observation narratives, coaching session summaries, educator self-reflections, and module feedback. The system must track the Fidelity Triangle distinguishing Confidence Gained from Behavior Change from Student Outcome Shift. The system must reveal the fidelity gap between confidence and behavior change, regional differences between Belize and California educators, which modules actually change behavior versus just build confidence, school-level factors explaining implementation success or failure, whether student progress links to teacher behavior change or just confidence, and scaling readiness indicators before expanding from UCLA pilot to full Belize deployment.
